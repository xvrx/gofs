# Guide to the Application of Double Taxation Agreements (P3B) based on PMK-112/2025

## Introduction

This document provides a comprehensive guide to the procedures for applying Double Taxation Agreements (Persetujuan Penghindaran Pajak Berganda - P3B) in Indonesia, based on the Minister of Finance Regulation No. 112/PMK.03/2025 (PMK-112/2025). This guide is intended for internal use by the Directorate General of Taxes (DJP) to ensure uniform understanding and application of the regulation.

This regulation is established based on the mandate of Article 50 paragraph (2) of Government Regulation No. 55 of 2022 concerning the Adjustment of Regulations in the Income Tax Sector. It replaces the previous regulations: PERDIRJEN No. 25 of 2018 and PERDIRJEN No. 28 of 2018.

---

## I. General Provisions (Ketentuan Umum)

This section covers the fundamental definitions and general provisions that form the basis of the entire regulation.

### **Article 1: Definitions**

Key terms used throughout this regulation are defined as follows:

*   **Double Taxation Agreement (P3B):** An agreement between the Government of Indonesia and a partner country or jurisdiction to prevent double taxation and tax evasion, which can be bilateral or multilateral.
*   **P3B Partner:** A country or jurisdiction bound by a P3B with Indonesia.
*   **DGT Form:** A form filled by a Foreign Taxpayer (WPLN) and endorsed by the competent authority in the P3B partner country for the application of P3B benefits.
*   **Competent Authority:** The official authorized to endorse the DGT Form based on the laws of the P3B partner country.
*   **Certificate of Domicile (SKD) for Domestic Taxpayers (WPDN):** A certificate issued by the Director General of Taxes confirming that a WPDN is a domestic tax subject as defined in the Income Tax Law.
*   **Certificate of Domicile (SKD) for Foreign Taxpayers (WPLN):** A certificate issued and endorsed by the Competent Authority of the P3B partner country confirming that a WPLN is a resident of that partner country for tax purposes.
*   **Special Form:** A form issued by the tax authority of a P3B partner containing a request for confirmation of the tax status of a WPDN in Indonesia.
*   **Agent:** An intermediary acting for or on behalf of another party.
*   **Nominee:** A party that legally owns assets or receives income for the benefit or based on the mandate of the actual owner or beneficiary.
*   **Conduit Company:** A company that receives benefits from a P3B on income from Indonesia, while the economic benefits of that income are owned by a party in another country who would not be entitled to the same benefits if the income were received directly.

### **Article 2: General Provisions**

*   **Income Tax Imposition:** Income tax is imposed on WPDN receiving income from outside Indonesia and WPLN receiving income from Indonesia in accordance with the Income Tax Law or the provisions of the relevant P3B.
*   **Application of P3B:**
    *   WPDN must use an SKD WPDN.
    *   WPLN must use a DGT Form.
*   **Requirements for WPLN to Obtain P3B Benefits:**
    1.  Not a domestic tax subject in Indonesia.
    2.  A resident of a P3B partner for tax purposes.
    3.  Does not misuse the P3B.
*   **Forms of P3B Benefits:**
    *   Lower withholding tax rates than those stipulated in the Income Tax Law.
    *   Exclusive taxation in the country of domicile.
    *   Exemption from income tax in Indonesia.
    *   Different determination period for a permanent establishment (BUT) than stipulated in the Income Tax Law.
*   **Purpose of P3B:** To eliminate double taxation without creating opportunities for non-taxation or tax reduction through tax evasion or avoidance.

## II. Procedure for Application of P3B by Foreign Taxpayers (WPLN)

This section details the procedures for WPLN to avail P3B benefits.

### **Article 8 & 21: DGT Form Provisions**

The DGT Form is a declaration by the WPLN that must be:
*   Filled in correctly, completely, and clearly.
*   Signed or marked with an equivalent sign by the WPLN.
*   Endorsed by the Competent Authority of the partner country.
*   Used for the period stated in the DGT Form, for a maximum of 12 months.
*   In the format specified in the appendix of PMK-112/2025.

### **Article 9 & 22: Endorsement of DGT Form & SKD WPLN**

An SKD WPLN can be used as a substitute for the endorsement of the DGT Form, provided it is in English and contains at a minimum:
*   Name of the WPLN.
*   Date of issuance.
*   Name and signature (or equivalent) of the Competent Authority.
The validity period is as stated in the SKD or based on the tax period of its issuance if no period is stated.

### **Article 10 & 11 & 23: Procedure for P3B Application**

*   **First Withholder:**
    *   Checks the DGT Form against books, records, and/or documents.
    *   Submits the DGT Form information electronically.
    *   Receives a receipt for the DGT Form.
    *   Provides the receipt to the WPLN.
    *   Withholds tax according to the Income Tax Law or P3B.
*   **Subsequent Withholders:**
    *   Check the information in the electronically submitted DGT Form.
    *   Check against books, records, and/or documents.
    *   Withhold tax according to the Income Tax Law or P3B.

### **Article 12 & 24: Exception for DGT Form Submission**

The following entities receiving income from Indonesia are exempted from submitting the DGT Form:
*   Governments of P3B partners.
*   Regional Governments of P3B partners.
*   State Agencies of P3B partners.
*   Central Banks of P3B partners.
*   Specific institutions explicitly mentioned and agreed upon in the P3B.
They may use an SKD WPLN or a Certificate of Registration instead.

### **Article 13 & 25: Procedure for P3B Application for Withholders under Article 12**

The withholder must:
*   Check the SKD WPLN or Certificate of Registration against books, records, and/or documents.
*   Submit the information from the SKD WPLN or Certificate of Registration electronically.
*   Withhold tax according to the Income Tax Law or P3B.

### **Article 14 & 26: Tax Withholder/Collector Obligations**

*   **If tax is withheld:**
    *   Pay the PPh.
    *   Report the PPh.
    *   Create proof of withholding.
*   **If no tax is withheld:**
    *   Report the PPh.
    *   Create proof of withholding.

### **Article 15 & 27: Tax Overpayment Refund (Refund)**

A WPLN can request a refund for overpayment of withholding tax through the withholder/collector, according to the regulations concerning tax overpayment refunds within the core administration system implementation framework.

### **Article 16 & 28: Document Retention Obligations**

*   **First Withholder:** Must keep the DGT Form, SKD WPLN (if any), and the underlying books, records, and/or documents.
*   **Subsequent Withholders:** Must keep the DGT Form receipt and the underlying books, records, and/or documents.

### **Article 17 & 29: DJP's Authority to Request Documents**

The DGT Form submitted to the Director General of Taxes during an audit, objection, or reduction/cancellation of a tax assessment letter can be considered as a basis for imposing Income Tax according to the P3B, as long as the WPLN meets the requirements of Article 2 paragraph (4).

## III. Procedure for Application of P3B by Domestic Taxpayers (WPDN)

This section outlines the procedures for WPDN to apply for P3B benefits.

### **Article 3 & 14: Delegation of Authority**

The Director General of Taxes has the authority to:
*   Issue SKD for WPDN.
*   Conduct research on applications for Special Form endorsement and mandate it to the Head of the Tax Office.
*   Endorse the Special Form and mandate it to the Head of the Tax Office.
*   Reject applications for Special Form endorsement and mandate it to the Head of the Tax Office.

### **Article 4 & 15: Application for SKD WPDN**

*   **Applicant:** A WPDN who has an NPWP and has submitted their annual tax return.
*   **Tax Year:** Can be for the current, previous, or a fraction of a tax year.
*   **Application:** One application per P3B partner, per tax year/period, and per transaction.
*   **Submission:** Through the tax portal or contact center.
*   **Content:** Must include name, identity number, physical address, email address, and an explanation of the income from the counterparty.

### **Article 5 & 16: Issuance of SKD WPDN**

*   Issued electronically and automatically if the requirements in Article 4 are met.
*   Valid until December 31 of the year of issuance.
*   The format is as specified in the appendix of PMK-112/2025.

### **Article 6 & 17: Application for Special Form Endorsement**

*   **Requirements:**
    *   Must be accompanied by the Special Form to be endorsed.
    *   Signed by the WPDN, their representative, or proxy.
*   **Form Content:**
    *   In English.
    *   Filled by the WPDN with at least: WP and NPWP name, WPDN status, P3B partner name, and the relevant tax year, consistent with the SKD WPDN.

### **Article 7 & 18: Endorsement and Rejection of Special Form**

*   The Head of the Tax Office conducts research on the fulfillment of the requirements.
*   The result is either an endorsement or a rejection of the application.
*   The process must be completed within 10 calendar days.
*   The WPDN can re-apply if the requirements are met.

## IV. Prevention of P3B Misuse (Pencegahan Penyalahgunaan P3B)

This section describes the provisions aimed at preventing the misuse of P3B.

### **Article 18 & 31: DJP's Authority to Test Compliance**

The DJP has the authority to test compliance with the fulfillment of withholding/collection obligations by applying the anti-abuse provisions of the P3B, which include:
*   **Beneficial Owner (BO)**
*   **Dividend Transfer Transaction**
*   **Indirect Transfer of Immovable Property**
*   **Limitation on Benefits (LOB)**
*   **Principal Purpose Test (PPT) / Main Purpose Test (MPT)**
*   **Avoidance of Permanent Establishment (PE) status**

If P3B misuse is found, the DJP will impose tax according to the Income Tax Law.

### **Article 19, 32, 33, 34: Beneficial Owner (BO)**

A party is considered a Beneficial Owner if it:
*   Is not acting as an agent, nominee, or conduit.
*   Has control over the assets.
*   Does not use more than 50% of its income to fulfill obligations to other parties.
*   Bears the risk of its own assets, capital, or liabilities.
*   Has no written or unwritten obligation to pass on the income received to another party.

### **Article 20, 35, 36, 37: Dividend Transfer Transaction**

To benefit from a lower tax rate on dividends, a WPLN must meet minimum share ownership percentage and holding period requirements (typically 365 days). Failure to meet these conditions will result in the application of a higher tax rate.

### **Article 21, 38, 39, 40: Indirect Transfer of Immovable Property**

Indonesia has the right to tax gains from the transfer of shares or interests in an entity if the value of immovable property in Indonesia exceeds a certain threshold of the entity's total assets, and this threshold is met at any time within a 365-day period before the transfer.

### **Article 22 & 41: General Provisions for Permanent Establishment (BUT/PE)**

A PE is a fixed place of business through which the business of a WPLN is wholly or partly carried on. This includes:
*   A place of management, branch, office, factory, workshop, etc.
*   Construction, installation, or assembly projects exceeding a certain time limit.
*   A non-independent agent.
*   An insurance agent/employee.
Activities of a preparatory or auxiliary character are generally not considered a PE.

### **Article 23 & 42, 43: Prevention of PE Avoidance (Construction, etc.)**

The periods of related projects carried out by the WPLN and its closely related persons at the same location are aggregated to determine if the time threshold for a PE is met.

### **Article 24 & 44, 45: Prevention of PE Avoidance (Non-independent Agents)**

A WPLN is deemed to have a PE if a person acting on its behalf habitually plays the principal role leading to the conclusion of contracts that are routinely concluded without material modification by the WPLN.

### **Article 25 & 46, 47: Prevention of PE Avoidance (Preparatory or Auxiliary Activities)**

The activities of a WPLN and its closely related persons are combined to determine if the overall activity is preparatory or auxiliary. If the combined activities constitute a cohesive business operation and are not merely preparatory or auxiliary, a PE may be deemed to exist.

### **Article 27 & 49, 50: Limitation on Benefits (LOB)**

This provision restricts the benefits of a P3B to entities that meet certain criteria, ensuring that only genuine residents of the partner country with substantial economic ties to that country can enjoy the treaty benefits.

### **Article 28 & 51, 52, 53: Principal Purpose Test (PPT) / Main Purpose Test (MPT)**

A P3B benefit will not be granted if obtaining that benefit was one of the principal purposes of any arrangement or transaction that resulted directly or indirectly in that benefit, unless it is established that granting that benefit in these circumstances would be in accordance with the object and purpose of the relevant provisions of the P3B.
